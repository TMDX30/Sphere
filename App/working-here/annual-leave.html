Annual Leave
Introduction

Much of the organisation operates on a fairly informal basis in terms of taking annual leave. Check the process in your area with you line manager when you arrive, but most people are able to send their manager an appointment for leave (probably best to give about a weeks’ notice) which will then be either accepted or rejected by your line manager. 99.9% of times, your manager will accept your leave but occasionally (for example during times of low-staffing) then your manager reserves the right to say no.

The office has also recently allowed staff to authorise Annual Leave on the Etarmis® system, so you won't have to wait for your manager to add your time back on. Managers do check this periodically and you can be disciplined for incorrect use of this, but your manager will explain all that to you once you join the team.

Full time

You will initially receive 25 days of annual leave. After each year of service you will receive an additional day until you reach a cap of 30 days. If you are transferring from another government department, your annual leave entitlement will depend on whether you are on modernised or pre-modernised terms and conditions and HR will be able to provide you with your entitlement before or when you join.

Part time

In broad terms you will get annual leave and bank holidays on a pro-rata basis. This means that our HR department will work out your leave and privilege days based on your hours.

Holidays

You will not be required to work bank holidays and will receive an additional day of privilege leave for the Queen’s birthday. Before booking leave, it is useful to have a chat to your manager about how much notice they usually like to have and if there are any reasons why your team might need cover at particular times. If you already have a holiday booked before you join us, it would be helpful to let HR know before you join, so that your new line manager knows about it.
